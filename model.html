Modeling with me: what to expect.

To begin, I would like to thank you for considering to work with me.

Feel free to review my portfolio.

This is a primer to the experience if I had approached you regarding modeling for me.

I have eyes for talent and am always looking for models of both genders and many walks of life.

Volunteer modeling is the most common. Chances are very good that if you have been approached by me, this is what was in mind. This is unpaid work for a gift print.

There is also modeling for a fee. This usually occurs when I have paid work that fits a model.

For paid work I always look to the models that have worked with me as volunteers first as they am most important to me at a number of levels.

Here am some of the things that happen at every photography shoot:

  1. You will be asked for proof of age. When a model is under the age of 18, parents or guardians must be made aware of the event, sign a release, and be invited to inspect the shoot.
  2. You have the right to refuse any pose. You will be treated with respect at all times and I assume responsibility for your safety.
  3. Before the shoot you will be given the opportunity to ask questions. Communication is highly important. The model- release contract will be explained to you. Again, if you have any questions, please ask them.
  4. After the I shoot you will be paid and will be asked to sign a release. Signing the release is to allow me to use the images as I need.
  5. You may bring a parent or guardian, friend, or other chaperon if you wish. It is ideal for everyone's comfort level.

Again, thank you for your consideration. Contact me.
